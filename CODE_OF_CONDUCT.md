# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, professional, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

Participants are expected to contribute in ways that support a healthy technical community, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, experience level, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful in technical discussions
- Giving constructive, specific feedback
- Assuming good intent while staying honest about problems
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Welcoming questions from contributors at different experience levels

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussion
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional engineering environment

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official communication channels related to this repository.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainer through GitHub or the repository's listed contact channel.

Maintainers will review and investigate reports fairly and may take any action they consider appropriate, including warning, temporary restriction, or removal from the project space.
